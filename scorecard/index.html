THE FIGHT BIG MONEY
SCORECARD

Issued by
From Ted Cruz to Bernie Sanders and Donald Trump to Hillary Clinton, presidential candidates from across the political spectrum have spoken out about our broken political system in which donors have too much influence with politicians and everyday people have too little. But so far, only a few of these candidates have offered real, concrete solutions to address these problems they criticize. Where does everyone stand?

The Fight Big Money scorecard will show you.

We have graded presidential candidates on their support for policies to give everyday people a bigger voice in politics as well as their efforts to champion these policies on the campaign trail and commitment to implementing them if elected.

How Are the Presidential
Candidates Graded?

Candidates earn credit for the following:
1.
Endorsing policies referenced in the Fight Big Money Agenda
2.
Championing the Issue
Making the issue of fighting big money a central theme in their campaign
Making these solutions to the problem a core part of their message throughout the campaign
Showing and/or declaring their commitment to taking action to advance solutions if elected
3.
Candidates who have not endorsed any policies in the Fight Big Money agenda can still earn some credit for supporting policies that are aimed at reducing the influence of big money in politics and that are not in contradiction with the Fight Big Money agenda.

Where Do They Stand?

Jeb Bush
Republican
Endorsed policies that attempt to reduce the influence of big money in politics and that are not in contradiction with the Fight Big Money Agenda:
1. Transparency: supports online disclosure of contributions, but it’s not clear the extent to what he thinks should be disclosed.
2. Lobbying: supports more disclosure of lobbying activities and lengthening the “cooling-off period” for former members of Congress becoming lobbyists

B+
Hillary Clinton
Democrat
Endorsed Fight Big Money Policies:
Everyone Participates
1. Endorses public financing for federal candidates, and has declared commitment to prioritizing and working with Congress to pass legislation creating a public funding plan for federal candidates.
Everyone's Voice is Heard
1. Has committed to work with Congress to pass legislation to reduce barriers to the ballot box and increase turnout.
2. Supports “meaningful” contribution limits.
3. Endorses reforms to modernize our voter registration system to make voting more convenient and secure. These reforms would provide Americans with the options they need to ensure they can register, vote, and make their voices heard.
4. Endorses restoring the Voting Rights Act.
Everyone Knows Who Is Trying To Influence Our Views And Our Representatives
1. Has committed to push Congress to enact new disclosure requirements for outside spending groups.
2. Has committed to urge the FEC to create dark money regulations responsive to the Citizens United decision.
3. Has committed to urge the SEC to require public corporations to disclose their political spending in her first 100 days in office.
4. Has committed to urge the FCC to require advertisers to disclose their “true identity” in her first 100 days in office .
5. Has committed to urge the IRS to more clearly define political activity so organizations cannot abuse the system to keep their donors secret.
6. Has committed to issuing an executive order requiring all federal government contractors to disclose their political spending in her first 100 days in office.
Everyone Plays By Fair, Common-Sense Rules
1. Has committed to endorsing, prioritizing, and calling on Congress to pass and the states to ratify the Democracy for All amendment.
2. Has committed to appoint justices who will transform the Supreme Court’s approach to money in politics by overturning Citizens United v. FEC and Buckley v. Valeo, and revive the People’s ability to protect our democracy.
Everyone Is Held Accountable, With Enforceable Penalties To Deter Bad Behavior
1. Has committed to endorse, prioritize, and work aggressively with Congress to pass legislation to create a new enforcement agency with real power to hold campaign violators accountable.
2. Has committed to endorse, prioritize, and work aggressively with Congress to pass legislation to shut down individual-candidate super PACs and strengthen the rules that prohibit coordination between candidates and outside spending groups.
3. Has committed to appointing FEC commissioners who promise to enforce existing law.
4. Has committed to appoint an Attorney General who will prioritize addressing violations of campaign finance and election laws.
Championing:
Making the issue of money in politics central to campaign
On January 21, 2016, Clinton published an op-ed in CNN entitled, “The cure for Citizens United is more democracy.”
On January 8, 2016, Clinton published an op-ed in The Boston Globe on Citizens United entitled “A make-or-break moment for Supreme Court appointments.”
Pushes out message via social media.
Making solutions to the problem a core part of message
Talked about solutions in an interview such as appointing Justices to the Supreme Court who would overturn Citizens United.
Talked about public financing for small donors and the importance of appointing Supreme Court Justices "who think a person's right to vote is more important than a billionaire's right to buy elections."
Talked about federal contractor disclosure, disclosure of political spending for outside groups, passing an amendment overturning Citizens United, small-donor public financing, and making it easier to vote.
